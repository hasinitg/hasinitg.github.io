Hasini Gunasinghe

huralali@purdue.edu

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Computer Science at Purdue University, where I focused on design and development of privacy preserving and secure online protocols for real world use cases, by combining in novel ways the advances in cryptography and computer science. In my Ph.D. thesis, I mainly addressed the use cases in the domain of digital identity management. My research has been supported by the Bisland Dissertation Fellowship, Emil Stefanov Memorial Fellowship, the IBM PhD Fellowship, Summer Research Grant awarded by Purdue Graduate School and Graduate Research and Teaching Assistantships by the Computer Science Department.

Before joining grad school, I have worked as a Software Engineer in WSO2 Inc. where I contributed to the open source WSO2 Identity Server product. My contributions have been recognized by the Outstanding Contributors' awards by the founders. I obtained my bachelor's degree in BSc (Hons) in Computer Science and Engineering from University of Moratuwa, Sri Lanka.
